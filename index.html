All of us have seen a dolphin, whether it be on a picture, in a dolphinarium, or even swam with them. It's no secret that they are very playful and gentle creatures, and there have been many stories of dolphins saving humans from shark attacks. We have been enjoying dolphin shows for 82 years now and counting; they're truly majestic and wonderful! But are they as wonderful as we think?

Have any of you ever wondered how the animals are treated outside of shows? If you have, you might have not given it a second thought and forgotten about it after. However, I got more invested into this, and researched the problem. Turns out, they are starved and separated from other dolphins until they learn their lesson, and have to play to live. Not as fun now, is it?

Luckily, in Canada it is now illegal to have dolphins and whales (ie. belugas and orcas) in captivity, so the problem is almost eradicated in the country. However, this doesn't mean that any sorts of bans/regulations have been passed out in other countries. For example, in Russia, there is absolutely no control over the capture and care of dolphins.

Some "fun" facts about dolphins

  1. Dolphins can't live without movement. In the wild, they swim around 100km through the ocean; they can't do that in a pool.


  2. The cases of dolphin suicides are rather high. They take every breath consciously, so if life becomes unbearable, they just stop breathing.


  3. In dolphinariums dolphins are injected with tranquilizers and antidepressants to prevent aggression of the animal due to stress.


  4. In the wild, dolphins live from 25 to 50 years old, but more than half of the dolphins die during the first two years of captivity. Their average life expectancy is 5 years.
